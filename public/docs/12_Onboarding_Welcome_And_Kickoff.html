First invoice paid / payment confirmed
Any existing roadmap, product strategy doc, or requirements document
Org chart or team structure (even a rough one)

How We'll Work Together

Primary Communication

Slack (preferred) or email. I'll join your workspace and respond within one business day to async messages.

Video Calls

Google Meet or Zoom, your preference. Calendar invites are sent from ename@doxaandco.co.

2-week sprints (default). Sprint planning on Day 1, review/retro on Day 14. Adjusted to your team's rhythm.

All deliverables written in your system of record (Notion, Confluence, etc.) — not in a format only I can access.

If something isn't working, say so early. I'd rather have an awkward conversation in week 2 than a failed engagement in week 8.

Kickoff Session Agenda

Send this agenda to all attendees 24 hours before the kickoff call. Duration: 60–90 minutes.

The kickoff is not a demo or a sales call — it's the first working session. Come prepared to share the real picture: what's broken, what's been tried, and what success actually looks like. Everything said here is confidential.

Attendees needed: The primary point of contact, any relevant technical leads, and ideally the executive sponsor or decision-maker. I'll take notes — you don't need to.

Agenda

1

Introductions & Context-Setting

10 minutes

Brief round of introductions — name, role, and what each person's stake is in this engagement. Enam shares working style, communication preferences, and how she approaches the first 2 weeks.

2

Current State Deep Dive

20–25 minutes

Walk through where things stand today. Not the polished version — the real one. What's working, what isn't, what's been tried and failed. For product engagements: existing backlog, technical architecture, team capacity. For GRC: current platform state, audit findings, compliance gaps. For AI: current tool stack, data readiness, team familiarity.

3

Define "Done" — Success Criteria

15 minutes

What does a successful engagement look like at 30 days? At the end of the contract? Defining this now means we have a shared yardstick — and it prevents "I thought this would include…" conversations later. Enam will document these criteria and share them before the next session.

4

Constraints, Risks & Hard Questions

10–15 minutes

What might get in the way? Budget limits, team bandwidth, organizational politics, technical debt, upcoming audits or deadlines? What's the thing nobody says in meetings? This is the conversation most consultants avoid in week one. We won't.

5

Tools, Access & Team Introductions

10 minutes

Confirm all tool access from the onboarding checklist has been set up. Introduce any team members who weren't on the call. Set up recurring sprint ceremonies if applicable.

6

Next Steps & First Deliverable

5–10 minutes

What happens in the first 7 days after this call? Enam will confirm the first concrete output — whether that's a backlog structure, a gap analysis, an AI readiness report, or a sprint planning session. No vague "we'll align on scope." Specific output, specific deadline.

Post-Kickoff: What to Expect

  • Within 24 hours: Kickoff notes and action items shared via email and your project tool
  • Within 48 hours: Confirmed sprint cadence or milestone schedule added to shared calendar
  • Within 7 days: First substantive deliverable delivered (per SOW)
  • Ongoing: Weekly async status update every Friday — what was done, what's next, any blockers
